Recipe for Lasagna

Description

This small dish lasagna recipe is a delightful, scaled-down version of the classic, ideal for intimate dinners. It layers no-boil lasagna noodles with a rich, savory meat sauce, creamy ricotta mixture, and a blend of melted mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses. Each bite offers a comforting taste of traditional Italian cuisine, baked to perfection in a compact dish that serves two generously. It's a simple yet elegant meal that promises all the flavors of a traditional lasagna without the fuss.

Ingredients

  • No-boil lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef (about 1 cup)
  • Onion (1 small, diced)
  • Garlic cloves (2, minced)
  • Olive oil
  • Crushed tomatoes (about 1 cup)
  • Italian seasoning (1 teaspoon)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Ricotta cheese (1 cup)
  • Egg (1, beaten)
  • Parmesan cheese (a handful, grated)
  • Fresh parsley (1 tablespoon, chopped)
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat Oven: Begin by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare Meat Sauce:
    • Heat a bit of olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
    • Sauté the diced onion and minced garlic until translucent.
    • Add the ground beef, breaking it apart with a spoon, and cook until it's browned and no longer pink.
    • Stir in the crushed tomatoes and Italian seasoning. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    • Let the sauce simmer for about 10 minutes to combine the flavors, then set aside.
  3. Mix Ricotta Layer:
    • In a separate bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, beaten egg, grated Parmesan, and chopped parsley. Mix well until you have a smooth, creamy mixture.
  4. Assemble Lasagna:
    • Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce at the bottom of your small baking dish to prevent sticking.
    • Place a layer of no-boil lasagna noodles over the sauce, breaking them as needed to fit the dish.
    • Spread half of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, then sprinkle a layer of shredded mozzarella on top.
    • Add another layer of meat sauce over the mozzarella.
    • Repeat the layers (noodles, ricotta, mozzarella, meat sauce), ending with a final layer of noodles. Top the last layer of noodles with the remaining meat sauce, and sprinkle generously with shredded mozzarella and some additional Parmesan cheese.
  5. Bake:
    • Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil (to prevent the cheese from burning).
    • Bake in the preheated oven for about 25 minutes.
    • Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown on top.
  6. Rest and Serve:
    • Let the lasagna sit for a few minutes after taking it out of the oven to set, making it easier to cut and serve.
    • Serve warm with a side of garlic bread or a green salad for a complete meal.

Enjoy your cozy, homemade small dish lasagna!
